Curiosity, Backed by Evidence

I’m a high school student who’s passionate about psychology—especially the small moments that shape choices, emotions, and motivation. What excites me most is pairing that curiosity with data: using evidence to understand people more clearly and communicate insights responsibly.

I’m drawn to questions like What influences decision-making under pressure? and How do environments shape behavior? Whether I’m working on a research project, building an app, or creating visual work, I aim to connect human stories with measurable patterns.

I’m especially interested in the intersection of behavioral science, technology, and design—creating work that feels thoughtful, trustworthy, and useful in the real world.

01 Youth Mental Health

HealingHues

Our project focused on developing a youth mental health app, guided by market research and trend analysis. Strengths include free access and anonymity, removing financial and social barriers to support. Opportunities lie in the growing demand for mental health tools among tech-savvy teens and the influence of social media in promoting mental wellness. Key threats include age restrictions and parental concerns about screen time and online safety.

This journey emphasized the importance of balancing accessibility, privacy, and engagement to create a meaningful, trusted platform that supports adolescent mental health in a safe and inclusive digital environment.

03 Behavioral Research

Project Langer

Researched mindfulness and investor decision-making under pressure; developed an STP app modeling scarcity-driven stress under Prof. Ellen Langer’s guidance at Harvard University. The project empirically evaluated how induced mindfulness improves choice consistency, satisfaction, and reduces post-decision regret in simulated, high-pressure consumer decision-making tasks.
